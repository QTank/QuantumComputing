•Superconducting quantum computers
Advantages

▪High Designability: superconducting qubits, including charge qubits, flux qubits, and phase qubits, offer a high level of design flexibility.

▪Scalability: superconducting qubits are prepared using semiconductor microfabrication processes, allowing for the leveraging of advanced chip-making technologies and the production of high-quality devices.

▪Easy to couple: superconducting qubits can be efficiently coupled using either capacitance or inductance, providing ease in creating interactions between qubits.

▪Easy to control: microwave control is compatible with the measurement of superconducting qubits, simplifying the control measurements.

Challenges

▪Short coherence time: decoherence time in superconducting qubits can be limited, impacting stability and reliability of quantum information processing.

▪Qubits not true 2-level systems: other unrelated quantum states exist, introducing complexities in their interactions.

▪Low temperature and advanced cryostats: Operating at sufficient low temperature, often close to absolute zero, is a requirement for superconducting qubits. The use of advanced cryostats maintains the condition of quantum coherence.
