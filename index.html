About Me

I am a second-year Ph.D. student at the University of Ghent, where I specialize in the fascinating intersection of quantum computing and bioinformatics.

In my current research, I focus on mRNA codon optimization and mRNA folding, delving into the application of quantum computing to address pressing challenges in bioinformatics. Specifically, my goal is to contribute to the development of mRNA vaccines, a field where quantum algorithms hold immense promise for optimizing sequences and enhancing vaccine efficacy.

The convergence of quantum computing and bioinformatics presents unprecedented opportunities. My work involves employing advanced quantum algorithms to analyze biological data, striving to uncover novel insights that can revolutionize vaccine design. I am particularly excited about the potential impact my research could have on the fight against infectious diseases.

I am also excited to explore the potential of quantum computing to address challenging problems across various fields that require supercomputing abilities. With quantum methods, I aim to tackle complex computational tasks in these fields. I am eager to collaborate with experts proficient in classical or quantum approaches within these fields. I seek to unlock new insights and solution.
