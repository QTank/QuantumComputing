Genome sequence assembly

Genome sequence assembly is to reconstruct the whole original genome sequence from smaller fragments. With sequencing technologies, the genome is broken down to shorter segments called reads, which can be merged to recreate the complete genome sequence.

The process of genome sequence assembly includes:

1. Sequencing: sequencing technologies, like Illumina, can generate shorter genome fragments from a long sequence.

2. Preprocessing: to clean raw data to make sure the data accurate and useful.

3. Overlap detection: to find the overlapping regions among shorter fragments. These overlaps can be used for reconstructing the original sequence.

4. Assembly: to merge the reads into the whole sequence through these overlap reads.

5. Annotation: to identify genes, regulator elements, and other functional regions.
